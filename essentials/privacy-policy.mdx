## 1. Introduction

### Purpose of This Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how zkPass (“zkPass,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) handles information relating to your use of our website https://www.zkpass.org (the “Site”) and our protocol and related services (together, the “Services”).

It describes what information we may collect, how that information is used, how it is safeguarded, and what rights you may have under applicable data-protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

### About zkPass

zkPass is a decentralized protocol that enables users to generate zero-knowledge proofs (ZKPs) from Web2 data sources through zkTLS.

The protocol is designed to allow individuals to prove facts about themselves—such as legal identity, financial information, educational records, or achievements—without disclosing or uploading the underlying documents. By default, zkPass does not collect or store personal data from users of the protocol.

### Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to:

- Visitors to the zkPass Site
- Users interacting with the zkPass protocol and developer tools, to the extent that technical data is transmitted
- Individuals engaging with zkPass through official communication channels such as mailing lists, forums, or community servers

This Privacy Policy does not apply to:

- Third-party wallets, decentralized applications (dApps), or blockchains that interface with zkPass
- External websites or services linked from the Site

## 2. Definitions

For the purposes of this Privacy Policy:

- **Personal Data**: Information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. Under GDPR and certain other laws, IP addresses and cookie identifiers may be considered personal data.
- **Non-Personal Data**: Information that does not identify an individual, such as aggregated analytics or anonymized logs.
- **Protocol Data**: Zero-knowledge proofs, zkSBTs, cryptographic keys, and related information generated and stored locally on a user’s device. Protocol Data is never collected by zkPass.
- **Site Data**: Technical information automatically collected when visiting the Site.
- **Processing**: Any operation performed on data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.
- **Services**: The zkPass Site, protocol, developer SDKs, and official community channels.

---

## 3. Core Privacy Principles

zkPass is built on privacy by design:

1. **Privacy by Default**  
   Zero-knowledge proofs are generated locally and never transmitted to zkPass servers.

2. **Minimal Collection**  
   zkPass does not collect or store identity documents, financial records, healthcare data, or sensitive credentials.

3. **Selective Disclosure**  
   Users disclose only required attributes without revealing underlying data.

4. **User Sovereignty**  
   Users control when and where proofs are disclosed.

5. **Transparency and Accountability**  
   zkPass aims to comply with GDPR, CCPA, Swiss FADP, and similar frameworks.

## 4. Information We Collect

We distinguish between **Protocol Data**, **Site Data**, and **Community Data**.

### 4.1 Protocol Data

- zkPass does not collect or store Protocol Data.
- ZKPs, zkSBTs, and cryptographic keys remain under user custody.
- zkPass is not a data custodian or identity provider.

When you access the Site, we may collect limited technical information:

- IP address and approximate geolocation

This data is used for security, analytics, and operational purposes.

### 4.3 Community and Voluntary Data

If you engage with zkPass via official channels, we may collect:

- Email addresses for newsletters
- Usernames and identifiers on community platforms
- Participation records for events, hackathons, or programs

### 4.4 Sensitive Data

zkPass does not knowingly collect special categories of data under GDPR.

### 4.5 Aggregated Data

We may use anonymized and aggregated data for analytics and reporting.
