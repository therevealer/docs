## Overview

**Version:** 1.0 – December 2025  
**Jurisdiction:** Swiss Association (Zurich, Switzerland)  
**Entity:** zkPass DAO Association  

**Governance Portal:** https://snapshot.box/#/s:daozkpass.eth

zkPass DAO is organized as a Swiss Association under Articles 60 et seq. of the Swiss Civil Code.

The Association is established as a non-profit governance entity with the purpose to:

- steward the zkPass protocol and its on-chain and off-chain treasury;
- safeguard the long-term interests of the zkPass community; and
- provide a transparent and accountable governance framework for protocol decisions, ecosystem incentives, and strategic direction.

The Association does not engage in commercial operations and does not operate as an exchange, investment fund, or custodian. Its role is coordination and governance, not profit generation.

---

## Governance Philosophy: Proof-Based Participation

zkPass DAO is founded on the principle that participation and influence should be earned through **verifiable contribution**, not capital dominance alone.

Traditional DAO governance has faced recurring structural challenges:

- governance capture through token concentration;
- sybil attacks and unverifiable identities; and
- meaningful contributors remaining underrepresented.

zkPass DAO addresses these issues by anchoring governance to both **capital and verifiable participation**, using zkPass proofs and reputation signals rather than social noise or wealth alone.

At the center of this framework is **$ZKP**, a coordination token used to:

- participate in governance decisions;
- align treasury deployment with ecosystem growth; and
- connect protocol development with community incentives.

Capital matters, but credibility, contribution, and verifiable action matter alongside it.

## Organizational Structure

Governance is structured into clearly defined bodies with scoped authority.

### General Assembly (Members / Token Holders)

- Comprises Association members and, functionally, $ZKP holders participating in governance.
- Approves major protocol parameters, treasury allocations, and structural changes.

### Core Contributor Committees

- Operate under a progressive decentralization model.
- Execute day-to-day operations and treasury mandates as approved by governance.
- Responsibilities transition over time to community-elected structures.

### Advisory Council

- External advisors providing non-binding guidance on risk, compliance, and strategy.
- Holds no unilateral authority over treasury or protocol contracts.

### Treasury Multisig Committee

- Controls the on-chain treasury via a **4-of-5 multisignature Safe wallet**.
- Signers are selected by governance and represent different functional domains.
- Role is strictly executory and bound by approved proposals.

This structure ensures no single founder or company controls the DAO, and all authority derives from governance decisions.

## Governance Process

### Proposal Initiation

Proposals may be submitted by:

- any address holding at least **100,000 $ZKP** at the applicable Snapshot block; or
- Core Contributors acting within their authorized scope.

Proposal rights are managed through Snapshot configuration and DAO-appointed roles.

Governance-Qualified Participants (GQPs) do **not** possess independent proposal submission rights by default and primarily participate through discussion and voting.

All proposals must meet DAO-defined quality, eligibility, and moderation standards.

---

### Discussion & Voting

- Minimum voting period of **7 days**.
- Community feedback via Snapshot or governance forums.
- Core Contributors may provide non-binding technical, legal, or security assessments.
- Proposal authors may clarify context without altering execution scope.

Voting occurs via Snapshot or on-chain mechanisms as specified per proposal.

Voting power is primarily token-weighted, with optional reputation-based mechanisms where explicitly defined.

---

### Execution

Once quorum and approval thresholds are met:

- execution is performed by the Treasury Multisig Committee or authorized executors;
- execution strictly follows the approved proposal; and
- all transactions are publicly disclosed and auditable.

Execution bodies hold no discretionary authority.

## Voting Model

### Baseline Rule

- **1 $ZKP = 1 vote** for standard governance actions.

### Proof-Based Governance Layer

zkPass introduces an optional reputation-informed layer:

- addresses may accrue verifiable contribution proofs;
- reputation may adjust voting weight or gate specific votes; and
- sybil resistance is enhanced without revealing personal data.

These mechanisms are opt-in, proposal-specific, and never override quorum or approval rules.

## Quorum & Approval Thresholds

### Proposal Classification

**Standard Proposals** include:

- non-critical parameter changes;
- ecosystem grants within approved budgets;
- contributor onboarding;
- community initiatives; and
- non-constitutional governance improvements.

**High-Impact Proposals** include:

- major treasury reallocations;
- changes to governance mechanisms or voting rights;
- amendments to constitutional documents;
- legal structure or jurisdiction changes;
- material $ZKP token changes; and
- dissolution or transfer of protocol control.

---

### Quorum Requirements

A proposal is valid if **either** condition is met:

- **≥ 4% of circulating $ZKP supply** participates; or
- **≥ 1,000 Governance-Qualified Participants (GQPs)** vote.

#### Governance-Qualified Participants (GQPs)

GQPs are zkPass-verified contributors demonstrating sustained, governance-aligned participation, including:

- verified content authors;
- protocol contributors and maintainers;
- ecosystem integration partners;
- DAO-recognized working group members; or
- participants meeting defined reputation or VRS thresholds.

Low-effort or purely transactional actions do **not** qualify.

Each GQP counts as **one vote for quorum only**.

---

### Approval Thresholds

Once quorum is met:

- **Standard Proposals:** simple majority (≥ 50%)
- **High-Impact Proposals:** supermajority (≥ 67%) and **token-based quorum required**

Participant-based quorum supports continuity but does not replace token safeguards for high-impact decisions.

<Callout type="info">
Exact quorum thresholds, participant definitions, and approval requirements are defined in the Governance Charter and may be adjusted through a valid governance process.
</Callout>

## Treasury Management & Controls

### Treasury Structure

- Secured via a 4-of-5 multisignature Safe wallet.
- Designed to balance decentralization and operational efficiency.

### Signer Composition

- Technology Representative  
- Operations Representative  
- Legal / Association Representative  
- Ecosystem Representative  
- Community Representative (DAO-elected)

### Controls & Transparency

- Execution only after DAO approval.
- Operational allowances are limited and policy-capped.
- All treasury addresses are public.
- Periodic reports disclose inflows, outflows, runway, and allocation categories.

Treasury funds are used exclusively to support the ecosystem.

## Compliance Positioning & Role of $ZKP

From a regulatory perspective:

- zkPass DAO is a non-profit Swiss Association.
- $ZKP is a governance and coordination token.
- It is **not equity**, **not a claim on profits**, and **not an investment instrument**.
- Participation is voluntary and contribution-based.

## Closing Statement

By anchoring governance in verifiable participation rather than assumption, zkPass DAO aims to set a higher standard for decentralized coordination — balancing decentralization, accountability, and regulatory awareness while remaining community-driven.
